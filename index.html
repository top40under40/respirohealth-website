The Gap

The system can only see patients who arrive.
We are building for the ones who don't.

Every existing TB screening tool — including AI-based ones — assumes the patient has already decided to seek care. The real gap is upstream: people who normalise symptoms, fear stigma, or can't reach a DOTS centre. That population is invisible to the entire health system.

2.6M
TB cases estimated to be missed globally each year — the people no existing tool can reach

747

The Approach

Push model vs Pull model.
A fundamental difference.

Existing AI screening tools use a push model — a trained health worker goes out to find the patient. Respiro is a pull model — the patient initiates screening themselves, privately, before they've decided to visit a clinic.

1

Patient opens WhatsApp

No app download. No registration. Works on any phone that already has WhatsApp — 500 million in India.

2

Records a cough

A 30-second guided voice note, in the patient's own language. Private. No health worker present.

3

AI analyses acoustic features

BiLSTM cough segmentation extracts acoustic features combined with brief symptom inputs. Risk score generated instantly.

4

Routed to nearest DOTS centre

High-risk patients are guided to their nearest DOTS centre for molecular confirmation. The system feeds into NTEP, not around it.

The Dataset

Confirmed molecular diagnosis.
Not symptom guesswork.

We are building India's first longitudinally tracked TB acoustic dataset linked to GeneXpert, CB-NAAT, and TrueNat confirmed molecular diagnosis labels — the evidence standard that makes our model clinically defensible.

🧬
GeneXpert

Molecular diagnosis labels

Every cough recording is linked to a confirmed GeneXpert, CB-NAAT, or TrueNat result — including Rifampicin resistance status for drug-resistant TB.

📍
207 DTCs

Four-state DOTS network

Target deployment across 207 District TB Centres in UP, MP, Rajasthan, and Maharashtra — covering approximately 42% of India's annual TB case load.
